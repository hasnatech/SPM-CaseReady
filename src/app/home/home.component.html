Cargill Case Ready Features

Case Ready is a full service meat program designed to replace a grocery store's butcher. Case Ready delivers the meat already cut & packaged to the store, so the store employees only have to worry about placing it on the shelf. The service uses data & shopper insights to determine customers' packaging preferences and adapt to trends in both culinary flavors & shopping habits. Cargill also creates shelf-space solutions based on data to recommend assortment, pricing, & promotion opportunities. Case Ready reduces labor costs and shrink loss from products being thrown away/sold at a discount. It also reduces the need for inventory space as the meat is delivered ready to go. Case Ready claims to reduce shrinkage by 35% & packaging costs by about 75%.

Case Ready's main "competitor" in this case is the current method of paying a butcher worker approximately $31,000/year, which has a much lower up-front cost than Case Ready, but you have a hunch that the client isn't factoring a lot of costs like labor and lost sales. Your job is to create a unique, creative value proposition by first identifying value drivers then calculating the value of the most important value drivers, and then communicating your value proposition to your prospective client, Green Grocers.



Activity 1: Identifying Value

Given the product clues, identify value drivers for your product. You will need at least 3 value drivers & 1 negative value driver to enter your responses, but you should aim for 5 value drivers.

Please keep in mind that any value driver you submit will need to be calculated in subsequent steps of this course.

Customer Facts
  • Green Grocers is located in an area that makes hiring challenging & they struggle with turnover in the meat department. Meat cutting is done 5 times weekly and takes about 6 hours each time. It also has to be performed by an employee that requires special training
  • The butcher program takes about 10 hours each week for an assistant manager to track inventory and determine what meat needs to be ordered, cut, etc
  • Green Grocers estimates that they could increase sales by .02% if they could predict when products would run out and avoid items running out of stock. A major concern of managers is that they fear they lose sales due to long wait times
  • Shrinkage (theft/spoilage) costs the client about 6% of sales
  • Their current butcher program requires a significant amount of space in the backroom and cooling storage. If they utilized case ready, they could reduce their current packaging budget of $.65/lb
  • While not every cut is being used by Green Grocers, they do make some profits from their trimmings, which comprise about 3.3% of volume purchased & are sold at $.87/lb, on average
